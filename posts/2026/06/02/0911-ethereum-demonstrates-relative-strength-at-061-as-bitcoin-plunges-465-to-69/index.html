Ethereum Demonstrates Relative Strength at -0.61% as Bitcoin Plunges 4.65% to $69,619

2026-06-02 09:11 UTC 2026-06-02 09:11 UTC Sophie Lambert · Digital Assets Research Editor

Published (UTC): 2026-06-02 09:11:00

Reference prices: BTC $69,619 路 ETH $1,975 路 XRP $1.2630 路 BNB $681.33 路 SOL $79.40

Volatility: BTC high (-4.65%) 路 ETH low (-0.61%) 路 XRP high (-3.36%) 路 BNB medium (-1.39%) 路 SOL medium (-2.19%)

The five-asset complex posts a complex average move of -2.44% this session, but the directional dispersion is unusually sharp: Bitcoin and XRP lead the sell-off with elevated volatility, while Ethereum acts as a clear defensive anchor, bleeding only 0.61%. This divergence signals that capital rotation from major caps into relative outperformers—and away from high-beta laggards—is actively reshaping intraday positioning.

Bitcoin: Anchor Under Heavy Selling Pressure at $69,619

BTC prints a 24-hour loss of 4.65% on high volatility, the largest decline among the five assets. The session low near $69,400 on both OKX and Binance marks a break below the prior $70,000 psychological support, with exchange dispersion minimal ($69,621.90 vs $69,616.44), confirming the move is broad-based rather than venue-specific. Elevated volume and wide intraday ranges suggest continued risk unwinding; any bounce toward $70,500 would face resistance from the newly established supply zone.

Ethereum: Low-Volatility Defense Anchors Altcoin Leadership

ETH slips just 0.61% with the lowest volatility in the group, trading at a tight $1,975.06 on OKX and $1,975.13 on Binance. This outperformance relative to Bitcoin—a -0.61% versus -4.65%—highlights a clear flight-to-quality within the altcoin space. The near-flat daily structure suggests that buyers are defending the $1,960 support zone aggressively, and the relative strength is drawing rotational inflow away from BTC and XRP.

XRP: High-Volatility Laggard Mirrors Bitcoin Weakness

XRP drops 3.36% on high volatility to $1.2630, closely tracking BTC’s directional pain. With matching elevated vol regimes, XRP offers no relative refuge. The OKX/Binance convergence at $1.26 reinforces a uniform sell pressure. Until BTC stabilizes, XRP remains vulnerable to further downside acceleration, with the next support level near $1.22.

BNB: Moderate Selling Hides Underlying Rotation Risk

BNB declines 1.39% on medium volatility to $681.33, an intermediate performance that neither defends nor breaks out. The move suggests that BNB is acting as a middle-ground asset in the current rotation—not suffering the full brunt of BTC-linked selling like XRP, but also not attracting the same defensive bid as ETH. The price maintaining above $680 provides a fragile floor, but a breakdown below $675 would signal capitulation.

Solana: Moderate Volatility, Underperformance Within Altcoins

SOL loses 2.19% on medium volatility to $79.40, underperforming ETH by a wide margin and even trailing BNB. The relatively larger drop compared to other non-BTC assets indicates that SOL is not part of the current altcoin rotation that is pumping ETH. The $79 area has served as a pivot; a close below $78 would confirm a bearish tilt, while a reclaim above $81 would necessitate a shift in sentiment.

Cross-Asset Synthesis and Risk Observations

Correlation remains high between BTC and XRP (both high-vol laggards), while ETH decouples notably as the session leader. The complex average move of -2.44% masks a bimodal distribution: BTC and XRP drag the average down, while ETH’s mild decline pulls the mean up. This type of dispersion is characteristic of tactical rotation rather than systemic panic. Exchange spreads are negligible across all venues, indicating orderly execution despite the elevated volatility. Traders should watch whether ETH can sustain its relative strength through the US session—if it breaks, the entire complex risks a catch-down move.
